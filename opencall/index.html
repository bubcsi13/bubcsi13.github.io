OPEN CALL
FOR VIRTUAL
CURATING


We believe that women artists from the Central and Eastern European (CEE) region are underrepresented in the global art scene and deserve more recognition from institutions and audiences than they currently receive.

The Open Call for Virtual Curating aims to support women artists from the CEE region by inviting curators and institutions to engage with their oeuvres featured in the database of the Secondary Archive. In this project, members of the International Association of Curators of Contemporary Art (IKT) are invited organize virtual exhibitions for the Secondary Archive's artists.

About the Open Call //

OVERVIEW

The Open Call for Virtual Curating offers an opportunity for IKT member curators to explore the possibilities of organizing exhibitions in the digital realm. Curators are invited to experiment with the format of virtual 3D exhibitions and create their projects in the digital twin spaces of leading art institutions supported by cutting-edge Walter’s Cube technology and its Virtual Curating platform. The winners will be awarded an $800 prize (one per host institution), and their exhibitions will be published and promoted through the organizers’ and host institutions’ social media networks.

About the Open Call //

GOAL

The Open Call aims to promote the integration and rediscovery of post-war and contemporary CEE women artists through the exploration of the Secondary Archive – a digital platform featuring artists from the CEE region (Albania, Belarus, Czech Republic, Hungary, Kosovo, Poland, Serbia, Slovakia, and Ukraine). Curators, scholars, and academic researchers are encouraged to explore the digital archive and contact artists highlighted on the platform during their preparation process. The final exhibition proposals should include at least one artist from the Secondary Archive’s platform.

HOST
INSTITUTIONS
The virtual host institutions offered their digital twin spaces for the applicants to virtually realize their curatorial concepts with the help of Walter’s Cube Editor software. Take a look around and choose the proper venue for your ideas!

About the Open Call //

APPLICATION PROCESS

1. Register as an active IKT member or start working with one if you are not yet.
2.Select the artists from the Secondary Archive platform you would like to include in your exhibition.
3. Conceive your curatorial concept.
4. Get in touch with the chosen artists, estate or gallery directly or through the Secondary Archive.
5. Select digital twin space from the list.
6. Collect hi-res reproductions or filling out a request form for any 3D object digitization.
7. Install your exhibition in the digital twin space via Walter’s Cube Editor software.
8. Submit your finalized exhibition via our submission platform.

About the Open Call //

EVALUATION PROCESS

1. The internal team of Walter’s Cube will screen submissions to determine whether they meet the open call requirements.
2. A selection committee consisting of representatives from IKT (1 pers.), the Secondary Archive (1 pers.), Walter’s Cube (2 pers.), and the host institutions (1 pers. each.) will review submissions and select one winner per host institution. Winning exhibitions will be published and promoted by the host institutions as well as the organizers.
3. A shortlist of exhibitions will be created for each institution, to be published and promoted on the partners’ websites and social media platforms.

About the Open Call //

KEY INFORMATION

Call: Open Call for Virtual Curating – Exploring Women Artists from Central & Eastern Europe.
Eligibility: IKT members only. Curatorial teams must include at least one IKT member.
Location: Exclusively online
Submission deadline: 15 November 2023 12.00 pm. CET
Submission requirements:
- Link of the virtual exhibition, completed via the WsC website - Curatorial statement (define max. word count and format + way of uploading) - CV (determine requirements for team applications)
Number of applications: One applicant can submit multiple exhibitions , but only one project per host institution.
Application fee: Free
Prize: $800 per host institution
